Manananggal
The Tale

Beginning

In a small coastal village bordered by mangroves and rolling hills, life moved at a gentle pace during the day. Fishermen returned with their nets full, children played near the river, and elders gathered under large acacia trees to share old stories. But as the sun dipped below the horizon, the cheerful atmosphere faded into uneasy silence. Lanterns were lit early, doors were locked, and parents called their children inside, for the legend of the Manananggal was deeply rooted in their traditions. They believed that when the moon was high and the wind carried an eerie whistle, the creature would take flight above their homes, searching for its prey. Though many villagers had never seen it, the fear of the unknown kept them cautious and alert every night.
