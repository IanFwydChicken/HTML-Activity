Behind the Legend

The legend of the Manananggal originates from Philippine folklore and tells of a terrifying creature that can separate its upper body from its lower half, sprouting enormous bat-like wings to fly through the night. It is said to prey on sleeping pregnant women, using a long, thread-like tongue to feed on unborn children or the vulnerable. The creature often appears during the full moon, lurking in remote villages or near the fields where people are alone. Villagers believed that it could be warded off with garlic, salt, or prayers, and that community vigilance could protect the homes. Beyond its frightening actions, the Manananggal legend serves as a moral tale, warning of hidden dangers, the importance of caution after dark, and the need for unity among neighbors to face threats that might be unseen.
