Manananggal

In the vast landscape of Philippine folklore, few creatures evoke as much primal terror as the Manananggal. A ghoulish, man-eating sub-type of the aswang, this malevolent spirit is most famous for its gruesome ability to sever its torso at the waist, leaving its lower limbs behind while its upper half sprouts massive, leathery bat wings to take flight. More than just a monster, the Manananggal serves as a chilling cultural symbol of the hidden dangers lurking in the night and the vulnerability of the home.

Legend Teaser

The Manananggal is one of the most feared creatures in Philippine folklore, known for its ability to separate its upper torso and fly under the cover of night. This ghoulish spirit preys on the vulnerable, particularly pregnant women, and lurks in remote villages or dark fields.
